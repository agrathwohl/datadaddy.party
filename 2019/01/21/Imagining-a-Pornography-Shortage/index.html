Pornography A Loving and Caring Industry?

datadaddy.party
2019-01-21

The pornography industry may be one of the most giving and selfless media verticals out there - if you take them at face value.

Today’s internet journeyman is quite aware of the pervasiveness of pornography across the web. The likelihood that you’ve accidentally stumbled onto pornography accidentally is almost as high as the likelihood that you’ve intentionally sought out pornography online. So whether or not you enjoy watching naked people fornicating, you’ve seen pornography online.

Pornography is so widely available online for a multitude of reasons, but the enticing nature of the content itself is not one of them. You have not likely come across the latest Marvel blockbuster film on a streaming video site without looking hard for it. But if I were to simply Google for a place to watch the latest 4K video content from – oh, I don’t know, BangBros or Naughty America?? – I’d be able to find what I was looking for right away.

The cause for pornography’s vast online dominance is instead something more naunced. Today’s porn companies take a decidedly innovative approach to the contentious issue of “intellectual property rights.” The porn industry does not look at the “tube sites” that host their content for free as a criminal enterprise like the MPAA does. Rather, the porn tube sites are simply a technological hurdle which they must overcome to remain relevant. Porn producers are not fueling an
international piracy-fighting industry worth billions of dollars annually like the film and TV markets are. They’re simply not trying to go after their customers like the other content producers are. What a novel concept!

Sure, companies may wish to fine PornHub’s viewers for watching their latest hot sauce festish porn without paying for it, but PornHub would fight that request tooth and nail. After all, revealing users publicly would be the biggest PR disaster for a porn streaming site in the history of porn streaming sites.

The only reason PornHub will reveal users publicly is when forced to by aggressive governmental bodies. Even then, the only identities at risk of being exposed are those of uploaders, not viewers. Until the global prudishness towards sex, nudity, and the like decrease substantially, nobody’s porn viewing habits are likely to be exposed by court order.

Still, Don’t Use Tube Sites

That doesn’t mean you shouldn’t avoid these porn tube sites like the plague.

PornHub has a lot of data on its users. Indeed, if the CIA was able to find Osama Bin Laden’s porn preferences after raiding his compound in Pakistan, there’s a high guarantee that even if you’ve never visited pornhub.com, they’ve got your personally identifying information in their databases. Like Facebook, porn is a global, world-wide phenomenon, and simply by virtue of the network effect, PornHub has a gosh-darn-frick-ton of information on you, and given their impressive
viewership statistics, that information is very likely to be embarrassing, should it be leaked to the public.

If a criminal hacker were to steal this data (chances are, some have already, not to mention those CIA & NSA folks) people could be in for a world of torment.
