Media Distributors: Cut Your 'UX' Team

datadaddy.party
2019-01-26

The “insights” provided by your user experience designers are largely unimportant and quite expensive. Worse yet, they’re illogical, bolstering confidence and legitimacy among a group of people that generate just about zero benefit for the organization they work within. If we do nothing to stop this scourge from expanding, it will take over corporate capitalism and lead to the downfall of scientific inquiry within the entertainment and communications industries.

UX and Its False Premises

Evaluating UX ideas involves hardly any empirical evidence. Qualitative analyses may only be assessed via qualitative methodology, and research in the field builds on dubious claims of a posteriori knowledge. In what type of blatantly absurd universe are we able to legitimately claim that human beings possess non-sensory understandings of the technology around them?

A Perversion of Critical Fields of Study

UX studies derive from human-computer interaction studies - itself, a largely important and valid study of the way we interrelate with machines. Where UX strays from common sense is its strong aversion to actionable data. Research in the field is largely non-instrumental, and employs no measurement or instrumentation sanity. Its studies are not empirical, and its explanations exist entirely within the field of psychological hedonics. In other words - it’s all a bunch of futzing with numbers, in an effort to explain a foregone conclusion - one which is very likely incorrect. UX tactics are designed explicitly to be consumed and performed in front of an audience, leading to the entire damned practice being little more than a way for unqualified psychology majors and art school drop-outs to get their nonsense spouted in front of people whose time is too valuable to be wasting on such nonsense.

Professional Fact Hiders

It seems to have never occurred to the UX researcher that all customers of their products utilize positivist, not constructivist, knowledge. Users who view media are doing so through a positivist lens. Therefore, the entire platform chain that enables the product to be studied in the first place - from the network connection provided by Comcast, to the monitor being used to display the information collected by this innane UX person - builds off the epistemological axiom that
human beings understand the world and its various subsumed properties a priori - not a posterirori.

Fire Any Executives Who Fall For It

A UX researcher trying to understand a platform’s user base is illogical. The UX researcher approaches things from a fantasy land of their own design, fighting the very truly evident properties of the thing they’re studying, all in an effort to change the discussion altogether. And since executives are used to a finer, more nuanced type of English language being employed, which UX researchers forgo due to their lack of respect for the spoken word, they aren’t able to stop this behavior in their tracks, because they are unqualified in identifying the problems that lay right in front of them.

UX researchers are born to an era where text messages replaced phone calls, and the lack of trigger warnings in online corporate copy took precedence over the very clear and present danger posed by the erosion of freedom of speech. We have only since compounded upon these serious problems due to so many executives’ laxadaisical approach to the detection of bullshit in their employees’ claims. This is a primary skill required of the successful media executive. Media people lie constantly, because media is purely a business built upon dishonesty and manipulation.

Executives who hire UX researchers to understand the quality of experience for your media distribution platform should be fired. With haste.
