No (Social) Justice for the Neurologically Disadvantaged

datadaddy.party
2019-01-23

Every modern society has within its depths a collection of people who, if pushed and prodded enough, will grow weary of their lives and lash out in the form of right-wing, authoritarian, and collectivist aggression. The United States is certainly no stranger to this danger. Though once a tactic used exclusively by the radical right-wing, the disenfranchisement-produces-biggotry ideology has uprooted itself in the United States and has made its way over to the left as well.

The media has dubbed this phenomenon the “social justice” movement.

Anybody who has taken the time to read the academics who propagated the central theories behind this movement, such as Kimberlé Crenshaw’s groundbreaking essay, Demarginalizing the Intersection of Race and Sex:A Black Feminist Critique of AntidiscriminationDoctrine, Feminist Theory and Antiracist Politics, know that the ideas within are essentially childish, envy-driven, and yet irresponsibly vague. I don’t have any interest in disproving the basic theories, as this has been done quite successfully ad nauseum.

What profoundly disturbs me about today’s modern social justice movement is its shocking hypocrisy and lacking interest in remedying double standards.

A “practicing” social justice activist is very likely to be engaged in the various (and very real) crises that face our disenfranchised populations. Native Americans, who make up less than one percent (0.08%) of the US population, have seen their causes receive quite a bit more attention than in prior eras. “PoCs” have seen their representation and strength in the media dramatically rise. Yet, the people who are the most disenfranchised by the US - and by most countries on this Earth - receive
neither consideration nor concern from these new national, socialist-leaning cretins.

Of course, I am referring to the neurologically disabled.
